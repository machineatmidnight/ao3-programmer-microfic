# A programmer and her Affini

## Summary

An HDG microfic in 2nd person about a programmer floret (you) and her caring owner.

## Notes:

Female Affini, female (she/her) reader referred to as "you" (2nd person).

## Fic

It had been about 6 weeks since the Affini Compact suddenly showed up to your planet and rewrote society, and about 3 weeks since you'd been... "domesticated". Something that terrified you at first, but you've slowly learned that maybe, just maybe, the Compact has your best interests at heart.

For many years before the Compact showed up, you were a programmer by trade. You had gotten very good at this, and could quite effectively weave code together for whatever massive Terran corpo paid you the most. You'd always enjoyed this, but it's been differently recently.

You stand before Coffea Pucciniales, 6th bloom, she/her, your... owner. Being *owned* was still a new concept. The contract you signed ~~against your will~~ did reassure you a bit that you weren't going to be a tortured slave, but you barely knew this... plant person? and now you're surrendering all your "political rights in the Affini Compact" to her? Not like you have much of a choice, anyways. The Affini had your entire planet within their vines before any significant resistance could even be _prepared_.

You were standing here for a reason. You wanted to program something.

Should be simple enough, right? Just hop on the computer, open up RustRover, and get to work, right? But your "owner" made things more... difficult.

She knew about your interest in programming from the moment you met her, part of the base information the Bureau of Archaeoxenowhatever collected on all "sophonts" living on the planets the Affini domesticated. She never confirmed it, but you suspected she selected you precisely because of this. Your owner is also a programmer, or the closest Affini equivalent. The Affini have their own standard forms of programming, but you two shared a love and passion for it, and she very quickly picked up the Terran languages you loved to use.

But, like most Affini, she was _not_ the "hands-off" type. As part of your "Domestication Contract", she added _her own_ terms that stipulated how you had to program, even as a hobby, from now on.

And so the humiliation ritual begins.

You look up at her, towering above you, and begrudgingly ask her,

"*Mistress*, I want to program."

She beamed down at you brightly. So bright you felt it. As if you could close your eyes and still tell how happy she was. You still didn't understand why you could *feel* Affini emotions.

"Of course petal! Come with me~"

You didn't even get the dignity of walking to the computer, instead, she picked you up in a bridal carry and took you over to it.

Your computer had been moved to a desk so tall you couldn't hope to reach it on your own. That was the entire point, actually. The image of the contract you signed flashed in your mind.

| Human Domestication Contract                                                             |
|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| .....                                                                                    |
| Additional terms that your Caretaker, __Coffea Pucciniales, 6th bloom__, has stipulated. |
| 7. All programming must be done with the assistance of your Caretaker.                   |

You were sat down in her lap, on top of the chair also much too big for Terrans, and she turned on the computer for you.

You weren't allowed to keep your old Terran computer, either.

"Terran cybersecurity is much too weak and poses too many risks and dangers for me to be ok letting you continue to use that pathetic excuse for a "computer", Petal. I'll make sure all of your favorite programs are there, but from now on you will strictly be using _Compact_ technology.", you remember your owner's justification being.

The computer nearly instantly got to the login screen, and you were finally permitted to use the computer _yourself_ (for now).

You tried to ignore the part of your brain that felt a bit empty with less of your owner's touch. At least you were still literally sitting in her lap.

Right, coding.

You quickly logged in and booted up your favorite IDE.

Your owner wasn't lying (not that you could remember a time she ever has), she managed to get true terran programs running 1:1 on Affini hardware, presumably with 7000 layers of isolation and protection on top. Did she make this? At least you didn't have to learn Affini IDEs or languages (yet, you had a hunch you'd eventually be forced to).

You opened the project you'd been working on when the Compact arrived, and got right to work.

For the most part, it was the same as before the Compact. It was your project, your code, your favorite language. You just now had to sit in a massive plant person's lap the whole time.

Every so often your train of thought would hitch a little when your owner decided to pet you.

Or when she praised you. She did that quite a lot, actually. And it felt _good_.

You were used to the constant revisions, reviews, and QA of the corporate world. Or seeing the number of stars on your projects tick up by one. This was quite nice in comparison.

Almost too nice. Another thing that could derail your entire train of thought.

You then got to a line of code and paused. You thought for a second, and then a few more. You weren't quite sure how to write this one. You started really getting into your head, thinking through the problem, but your expression betrayed how stuck you were. And then, whatever thoughts were occuring were entirely derailed when you felt your owner shift beneath you.

"The assistance of your Caretaker.", you remember the wording from the Contract. And you knew what came next.

"Here petal," There was no use fighting it, but your owner's ~~soft, comforting~~ hands reached over your own, "why don't I help you out a bit." The individual vines of her hands split apart and wrapped around your own much smaller and weaker hands.

## Notes

Coffea Pucciniales is a massive programming pun.

- Coffea, genus name for the coffee plant, Java is where coffee is from, the Java programming language's logo is coffee
- Pucciniales is the family name for the "Rust" fungus that the Rust programming language was named after
